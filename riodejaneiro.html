RIO DE JANEIRO

Life is better when you’re in Rio!
There’s a reason it’s called the “Marvellous City.”

Rio de Janeiro is the second largest city in Brazil, on the South Atlantic coast. Rio is famous for its breathtaking landscape,
its laid back beach culture and its annual carnival.Rio - the most attractive tourist city in South America, a place that is a
symbol of fun, games, great atmosphere.Today, the former capital of Brazil and the Portuguese Empire is the center of
the most famous carnival in the world.This destination guards the most giant statue of Christ and boasts one of the most
famous beaches in the world: Copacabana.

HOW TO GET THERE
By Plane Galeão Airport--Most major airlines fly to Rio de Janeiro, sometimes with a stop or connection in São Paulo. International passengers arrive at Antônio Carlos Jobim Airport.
By Bus All long-distance buses arrive at the Novo Rio Rodoviaria, Av. Francisco Bicalho 1, Santo Cristo, close to downtown near the old port. It's best to use a taxi traveling to or from the station.
By Cruise Ship Cruise ships dock in the terminal almost opposite Praça Mauá. Downtown is an easy walk, and public transit is close by. If returning to the ship after dark, it's best to take a taxi. Praça Mauá becomes a somewhat seedy red-light district in the evening.

5 Can’t-Miss Foods in Rio de Janeiro

Feijoada
Where to find the best Feijoada in Rio: Cantinho do Senado, Feira do Lavradio, or anywhere in Ipanema.

Churrascaria
Where to find the best Churrascaria in Rio: Verne Provenzano, Gaúcha Churrascaria, Churrascaria Palace

Pão de queijo with Requeijao
Where to find Pão de Queijo in Rio: Cafe Cultivar, Casa do Pao de Queijo, Cafe Do Alto, Restaurante Glouton.

Coxinha de Galinha
Where to find the best Coxinha de Galinha in Rio: Bar Bracarense, Botequim Informal, Fornalha, Bar Teto Solar, Portella.

Brigadeiro
Where to find the best Brigadeiro in Rio: Capim Santo, Las Chicas Gourmet-Garage, Praça São Lourenço, Brigadeiro Doceria.

Seasonal Highlights

Spring
Considered the most enjoyable time of year Spring temperatures range from 70-90 F. The humidity is quite low but the days are very sunny. The mountains around the city are bloom with colorful wildflowers. 		Summer
Summer is the busiest time of year in Rio. Things start to heat up in October and by December, temperatures can reach 104 F.Usually it rains in the afternoons after a really hot day. Only a strong sunscreen, sunglasses and a hat will protect fair skin from the sun’s rays.
Autumn
When the amendoeira trees turn to red and yellow and the nights are cooler then autumn has arrived. Autumn runs from March through May. Temperatures run in the low 80s during the day and the 70s at night.		 Winter
June, July and August are the winter months in Rio. Temperatures can drop to 70 F and the wind and rain can really bring a chill to the air. The locals call this period ‘veranico’ meaning mini-summer.

Anytime is perfect to come to Rio. If you like hot weather visit in the summer or if sweating is not your favorite sport then try a winter vacation in Rio.
