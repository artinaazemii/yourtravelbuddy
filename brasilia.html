Brasília

The old imperial capital and cultural heart of Brazil

Brasília, city, federal capital of Brazil. It is located in the Federal District carved out of Goiás state on the central plateau of Brazil. At an elevation
of some 3,500 feet (1,100 metres), it lies between the headwaters of the Tocantins, Paraná, and São Francisco rivers.It was founded by President
Juscelino Kubitschek on 21 April 1960, to serve as the new national capital. Brasília is estimated to be Brazil's third-most populous city.Brasília,
conceived as a workable, utopic answer to urban chaos, replaced Rio as capital in 1960 and remains an impressive monument to national initiative.
Brasília is remarkable for the modernist architecture and artistic urban planning of Oscar Niemeyer and Lucio Costa. In 1987 UNESCO declared
the whole city a World Heritage Site.

HOW TO GET THERE
By Plane: The Brasilía International Airport (BSB) is just 7 miles from the city center and is a major international hub. Catch an Executive Bus from the airport to downtown for just R$10 or get a taxi for R$30-40
By Car: If you're coming in from the south or any of the central-western states, take the Saída Sul entrance. If you're driving in from any of the other states, take the Saída Norte.
By Bus: Brasilia's main bus station is Rodoviária, which connects to other major cities such as Rio De Janeiro, Belo Horizonte, and São Paulo.

Facts you probably didn’t know about Brasília

  • The city is located at the top of the Brazilian highlands in the country’s center-western region.

  • As of May 2019, the population of Brasília is about 3 million people. It is the 3rd most populous city in Brazil.

  • Brasília is the capital city of Brazil

  • The city covers a total area of 5,802 square kilometers (2,240 square miles).

  • Brasília lies at an elevation of 1,172 meters (3,845 feet) above sea level.

4 Can't-Miss Foods in Brasília

Carne de sol
Is traditionally prepared with beef, and the origins of this centuries-old meat preservation method are attributed to the so-called sertanejos, cattle ranchers living in the vast, semi-arid Brazilian hinterlands.

Cachorro quente
Is the Brazilian version of a hot dog consisting of a bread roll filled with sausage links, ground beef in a chunky tomato sauce with green peppers and onions, and melted cheese.Delicious Brazilian hot dog.

Rabanada
Is the Brazilian version of french toast. It's made with round or oval stale bread cut in thick slices that's dipped in milk and beaten eggs, fried in oil, then sprinkled with cinnamon sugar.

Churrasco
Is a Brazilian barbecue method where juicy pieces, slices, steaks, and chops of beef, veal, lamb, pork, and chicken are placed on big skewers and grilled over wood fire.

Brasília has two well defined seasons: the dry season and the wet season.

Dry Season
The dry season begins in May – a wonderful month to visit Brasilia; air temperatures are pleasant and the gardens and the city are more beautiful. In the dry season, everyone suffers from the weather.

Wet Season
The month with the most rain in Brasília is December, with an average rainfall of 8.6 inches. The rainless period of the year lasts for 2.8 months, from May 27 to August 22. The month with the least rain in Brasília is July, with an average rainfall of 0.1 inches.

The best time to visit Brasília goes from May to mid-August, being the coolest within the dry season. At night it can get a little cold, especially from June to August. From mid-August to late September, it is still the dry season, but rainy days become slightly more frequent, moreover, the likelihood of very hot days increases.
