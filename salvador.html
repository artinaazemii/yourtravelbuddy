Salvador

There's no time to be bored in El Salvador

El Salvador, country of Central America. El Salvador is the smallest and most densely populated of the seven Central American countries. It is bordered
on the northeast by Honduras, on the northwest by Guatemala, and on the south by the Pacific Ocean. El Salvador's capital and largest city is San
Salvador. The country's population in 2022 is estimated to be 6.5 million.The smallest country in Central America geographically, El Salvador has
the fourth largest economy in the region.Known as the Land of Volcanoes, El Salvador has frequent earthquakes and volcanic activity.
It is the only country in Central America that does not have a coastline on the Caribbean Sea.

HOW TO GET THERE
By plane: Salvador's Deputado Luis Eduardo Magalhães International Airport (SSA) is the main Bahian airport. Flights from all over the country arrive here as well as flights from the rest of South America, Europe and the U.S.
By Bus: There are a lot of bus companies such as São Geraldo, Itapemirim, Águia Branca, Gontijo, Real Expresso and Bomfim that have daily buses connecting Brazils major cities with Salvador. From São Paulo, there are buses on a regular basis. The approximate travel time is about 30 hours and ticket costs around R$ 200.
By Ferry Boat: The ferry boat linking Salvador to Bom Despacho, in Itaparica Island, allows shorten in 100 miles the distance between the state capital and the southern beaches of Bahia. There is a ferry every hour, from 05:00 to 23:30.

6 Can't-Miss Foods in Salvador

Pupusas
Pupusas are tortillas or thick griddle cakes made of corn or rice dough that are filled with several ingredients such as cheese, beans and pork rinds.

Corn Atole
Corn atole, also called atolli, is a hot beverage which, as its name indicates.It is made during the sweet corn season and can be found at affordable prices.

Torrejas
Torrejas is a sweet traditional Easter dish prepared using bread slices which are coated in eggs. They are then fried and soaked in the piloncillo syrup.

Tamal Pisque
Is a traditional dish prepared using seasoned corn masa which is combined with refried beans. The mixture is carefully wrapped in plantain leaves which are then steamed.

Yuca Sancochada
Yuca Sancochada is a dish made from cassava and fried pork rind. It is typically is served on a plate with an orchard leaf below the yucca.

Pastelitos
Pastelitos are small pancakes made of corn dough, seasoned with a little achiote. Inside these pancakes, there is usually a filling of ground beef.

Seasonal Highlights

Spring
March and April are two last months of dry season. At this time air in San Salvador warms up to about 31 °C (88 °F). Precipitation occurs from time to time but its amount is insignificant. Caribbean air masses are traveling across the isthmus and reach the country in hot and warm state.		 Summer
Summer is the heist of rainy season. There are no changes in temperature ranges however shower are occurring almost every day. At midst of July showers are gaining highest intensity and monthly amount of precipitations reaches 320 mm
Autumn
Weather of September bears all features of rainy season. It is hot, rainy and unbearable. In addition severe precipitation causes flooding and mudflows, damp dirt roads and other kinds of natural disaster. At October precipitation gradually decreases and by the beginning of November weather obtains features of dry season.		 Winter
At December last remnants of rainy season are gone and weather is perfectly fine. Range of annual temperatures is narrow thus winter is as warm as any other season. However absence of rains is reason why winter is the best time to visit the country.

The best time to go to El Salvador is in the drier, cooler months of November to March, known here as the summer. The wet season provides excellent surf conditions though.
