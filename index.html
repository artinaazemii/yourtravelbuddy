Brazil is the largest country in South America and the fifth largest nation in the world. It forms an enormous triangle on the eastern side of the continent
with a 4,500-mile (7,400-kilometer) coastline along the Atlantic Ocean.It has borders with every South American country except Chile and Ecuador.The
Brazilian landscape is very varied. It is most well known for its dense forests, including the Amazon, the world's largest jungle, in the north.But there are
are also dry grasslands (called pampas), rugged hills, pine forests, sprawling wetlands, immense plateaus, and a long coastal plain.Northern Brazil is
dominated by the Amazon River and the jungles that surround it. The Amazon is not one river but a network of many hundreds of waterways.Its total
length stretches 4,250 miles (6,840 kilometers). Thousands of species live in the river, including the infamous piranha and the boto, or pink river dolphin.
